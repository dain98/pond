# Product vision

## Premise

Pond is a virtual third place: a welcoming online environment where people can
spend time together without needing to compete or constantly pursue goals.
Fishing gives conversation a gentle rhythm, but the people are the reason to
stay.

Each server represents a small settlement. Its shared town contains a lounge,
bar, cafe, game room, pond, and other communal spaces. Persistent servers can
develop their own culture, appearance, residents, and traditions.

## Product pillars

### People

Identity, outfits, emotes, profiles, friendships, and presence make other
players feel expressive and recognizable.

### Places

The commons gathers everyone together. The pond supports parallel conversation
and play. Homes offer privacy and self-expression without replacing the public
town.

### Activities

Activities provide something to do while socializing. Fishing comes first;
cards, board games, arcade games, trivia, and similar diversions can be added
after the central experience works.

### Possessions

Fish, furniture, clothing, photographs, trophies, and keepsakes should be fun
to display and discuss. They are social objects, not merely statistics.

### Moments

Weather, time of day, visitors, shared discoveries, and small events create
memories without relying on scheduled chores or fear of missing out.

## Design principles

- Socializing is always a valid way to play.
- Activities should leave enough attention available for conversation.
- Joining friends should be quick and understandable.
- Progression should unlock expression, stories, and variety rather than power.
- Public spaces should remain useful so housing does not empty the town.
- Players should not be punished for missing days.
- The experience should remain responsive on modest desktop hardware.
- Servers should feel like communities rather than disposable matchmaking
  rooms.

## Explicit non-goals for the first version

- A massively multiplayer world
- Combat
- A global player economy
- Real-money or cash-equivalent gambling
- Mobile, browser, or console support
- Voice chat
- User scripting or unrestricted uploaded content
- Large procedural environments
- Live-service daily chores

## Emotional target

A player walks home after fishing at night, passes the illuminated cafe, and
sees a few familiar people still talking through its windows. Pond should feel
like somewhere one can return to, not a queue for the next match.
